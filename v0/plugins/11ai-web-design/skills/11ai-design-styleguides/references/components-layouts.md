# Components and layouts

## Page modes

**Root/editorial:** centered hero, restrained badge row, strong pitch, primary
action plus outline secondary action, generous section separators, then the
main catalog. Do not let marketing displace the product/data list.

**Non-root/analytical:** compact breadcrumb and metadata row, 28–40px heading,
short summary, actions inline, then filters/table/charts above the fold. Reduce
hero and section padding, card padding, control height, and prose width.

## Navigation

Use a quiet persistent header, breadcrumbs at every drill-down level, exact
source deep links, and clear up/down hierarchy. Keep labels concrete. On mobile,
collapse secondary navigation without hiding the current location.

## Catalogs and filtering

Lead with search and result count. Use compact filter chips, selects, or a
popover for secondary filters. Persist state in URL parameters. Provide reset,
active-filter summary, sort, and view/column controls. Empty states explain how
to recover.

Cards suit scanning varied content; tables suit comparison. Do not place a card
inside a card merely for styling. In compact mode default to table/list and use
expandable rows for secondary metadata.

## Metrics and tables

Align numbers right, use tabular mono, keep units in headers or values, expose
missing as `—` with explanation, and preserve provenance in tooltips/details.
Sticky headers and first columns are useful only when they improve orientation.
Support horizontal scrolling without clipping controls.

## CTAs

Use one filled primary and one outline secondary action per decision point.
Repeat only at natural completion points. Distinguish actions by outcome, not
generic labels. Terminal blocks are appropriate for commands and reproducible
developer actions; include copy feedback and never masquerade prose as a shell
command.

## Content and copy

Use short active sentences, concrete labels, real counts, and direct caveats.
Do not invent social proof or placeholder metrics. Put methodology and full
provenance behind progressive disclosure, not out of reach.

## Responsive behavior

Design mobile intentionally: reorder summary before secondary controls, turn
dense grids into horizontal-scrolling tables or stacked comparison rows, keep
tap targets usable, and preserve exact data. Do not merely shrink desktop.
